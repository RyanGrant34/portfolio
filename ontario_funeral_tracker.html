Investigation

Mapping Consolidation in Ontario's Death-Care Industry

Ontario's funeral industry is undergoing rapid, largely invisible consolidation. Birch Hill Equity Partners, a Toronto-based private equity firm, partnered with Iowa-based Homesteaders Life Company — a preneed life insurance specialist — to acquire Park Lawn Corporation in 2024 for C$1.2 billion, taking Canada's largest publicly-traded funeral company private. Park Lawn now controls 51 Ontario funeral homes under brands including Turner & Porter, Arbor Memorial, Kelly, Hulse Playfair & McGarry, Tubman, and Ward.

"Park Lawn is the largest publicly traded Canadian-owned funeral, cremation and cemetery provider" — and is now privately held by PE and a US insurance conglomerate.

Separately, Houston-based Service Corporation International (SCI) — the world's largest funeral company (NYSE: SCI) — operates 9 Ontario locations under its Dignity Memorial brand (Cardinal Funeral Homes, Kane-Jerrett, Jerrett Funeral Homes) and the Mount Pleasant Group (Mount Pleasant, Humphrey-Newbigging).

This tracker maps all 553 active, licensed funeral establishments in Ontario, sourced directly from the Bereavement Authority of Ontario (BAO) public registry. Every establishment has been geocoded and classified by ownership: PE-backed, corporate, OFSA-verified independent, or independent. PE and corporate homes together represent 10.8% of licensed establishments — concentrated in the highest-volume urban markets.

Questions to Ask Before Choosing a Funeral Home

You have a right to know who owns the business you are entrusting with your loved one. In Ontario, corporate and PE-owned homes are not required to disclose their ownership at point of service.
  • Is this funeral home independently owned, or part of a larger corporate group?
  • Has ownership changed in the last 5 years?
  • Are prices set locally, or by a head office elsewhere?
  • Can you see a General Price List before making any decisions?
  • Will the same staff serve your family, or is there high turnover?
  • Is this home licensed by the BAO? (Verify at portal.thebao.ca)
  • Does the home belong to the OFSA (Ontario Funeral Service Association)?

Know Your Rights in Ontario

Ontario's Funeral, Burial and Cremation Services Act, 2002 requires all funeral homes to provide a General Price List on request, display licence information, and obtain written consent before providing services. The BAO regulates and inspects all licensed funeral establishments.

If you have a complaint about a funeral home, contact the Bereavement Authority of Ontario at thebao.ca

Methodology & Data Sources

How This Data Was Built

Registry data was scraped from the Bereavement Authority of Ontario (BAO) public registry at portal.thebao.ca in March 2026, capturing all active Funeral Establishment Operator licences (Class 1 & Class 2). The portal runs on Microsoft Dynamics 365; data was extracted via Playwright browser automation.

Classification methodology:

  • PE-Backed: Name-pattern matched against Park Lawn Corporation's verified brand roster, with city-level disambiguation for ambiguous names (e.g. Armstrong Funeral Home — Oshawa = PE, Port Colborne = independent).
  • Corporate: Matched against known SCI/Dignity Memorial brands (Cardinal, Kane-Jerrett, Jerrett) and Mount Pleasant Group (Mount Pleasant, Humphrey-Newbigging, R.S. Kane).
  • OFSA Member: Cross-referenced against the Ontario Funeral Service Association public membership directory.
  • Independent: All remaining active licensees not matching the above categories.

Geocoding was performed via Nominatim (OpenStreetMap), achieving 552/553 successful geocodes (99.8%). One establishment could not be geocoded and does not appear on the map.

Ownership context sourced from SEDAR+ public filings, Park Lawn Corporation investor relations materials, SCI annual reports, and news coverage of the Birch Hill/Homesteaders acquisition.
